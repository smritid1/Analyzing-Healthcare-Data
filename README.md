# 🏥 Healthcare Efficiency Analysis Dashboard | Power BI

> A comprehensive Power BI case study analyzing New York State hospital discharge data to identify opportunities for improving healthcare efficiency, reducing patient Length of Stay (LOS), and minimizing treatment costs

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Healthcare organizations strive to provide high-quality patient care while reducing unnecessary operational costs. One of the most important indicators of hospital efficiency is the **Length of Stay (LOS)**, which measures the number of days a patient spends in the hospital.

This project analyzes hospital discharge data for **elective hip replacement surgeries** across New York State. The objective is to identify factors that influence patient LOS, hospital costs, and operational efficiency using Power BI dashboards and DAX.

The dashboard was created for a fictional healthcare consulting company called **HealthStat**, helping stakeholders make data-driven decisions to improve hospital performance.

---

# 🎯 Business Objectives

The dashboard aims to answer the following business questions:

- Which hospitals handle the highest number of patients?
- How are patient discharges distributed by age and gender?
- Which patient groups stay longer in hospitals?
- Which hospitals have higher average treatment costs?
- How do hospitals compare in terms of discharge volume and efficiency?
- Which clinical factors contribute most to higher hospital costs?

---

# 🗂 Dataset Information

The dataset contains **New York State Hospital Discharge Data** for patients who underwent **Elective Hip Replacement Surgery**.

Each record represents one hospital stay.

### Key Fields

- Hospital Name
- Health Service Area
- Age Group
- Gender
- Length of Stay (LOS)
- Total Cost
- Severity of Illness
- Risk of Mortality
- Diagnosis Description
- Patient Disposition
- Surgical Program Size

The dataset does not contain personally identifiable patient information.

# Dashboard Pages
- Identifies regions with the highest concentration of hospitals.
- Helps compare healthcare coverage across different service areas.
- Female patients account for a slightly larger share of hip replacement surgeries.
- Patients aged **50–69 years** represent the majority of hospital discharges.
- Older age groups contribute fewer discharges.
- Older patients generally experience longer hospital stays.
- Female patients have a slightly higher average LOS than males.
- Longer LOS indicates increased resource utilization and hospital costs.
- Some hospitals treat significantly more patients than others.
- High discharge volume does not necessarily indicate lower LOS.
- Enables benchmarking hospital performance.
- Certain hospitals consistently report higher treatment costs.
- Hospitals with longer LOS generally have higher treatment costs.
- Conditional formatting quickly identifies high-cost facilities.
- Extreme severity patients incur significantly higher treatment costs.
- Higher mortality risk increases average hospital expenses.
- Larger surgical programs generally demonstrate better cost efficiency.
- Patient disposition has a strong impact on total hospital costs.

# Key Findings

- Patients aged 50–69 account for the highest discharge volume.
- Female patients underwent slightly more hip replacement surgeries.
- Older patients tend to remain hospitalized longer.
- Increased Length of Stay leads to higher treatment costs.
- Hospital performance varies significantly across New York State.
- Patient severity and mortality risk are major cost drivers.
- Hospital efficiency differs considerably between facilities.

# 📚 Healthcare Terminology

### Length of Stay (LOS)

The number of days a patient remains admitted in a hospital.

---

### Discharge

When a patient leaves the hospital after treatment.

---

### Patient Disposition

The patient's destination after discharge.

Examples:

- Home
- Rehabilitation Center
- Skilled Nursing Facility

---

### Severity of Illness

Indicates how serious a patient's condition is.

Levels include:

- Minor
- Moderate
- Major
- Extreme

---

### Risk of Mortality

Measures the likelihood of patient death based on clinical condition.

Higher risk generally requires greater healthcare resources.

---

### Elective Surgery

A planned surgical procedure rather than an emergency operation.

Example:

Hip Replacement Surgery
